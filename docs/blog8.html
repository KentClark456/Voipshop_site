No Line Rentals. No Hidden Fees. Why VoIP Is Just Cheaper.

Traditional telecoms have conditioned businesses to accept line rentals, call-out fees, and confusing usage costs as the norm. But those days are over. Cloud-based VoIP removes the middlemen, the infrastructure, and the monthly “just-because” charges — leaving you with a cleaner, simpler, and cheaper solution.

Why Are There No Lines?

In the old model, every extension needed a copper or fibre line physically connected to your premises. You’d pay for the physical line, even if you weren’t making calls.

VoIP runs entirely over your internet connection. It uses the bandwidth you already pay for — so there’s no need for a separate “voice line.”

Line Rentals Are a Legacy Tax

Line rental isn’t a service — it’s a leftover from the infrastructure-heavy days of fixed-line telecoms. Most providers still charge you just to keep the line “active,” even if you're barely using it.

VoIP doesn’t need that. There are no extra ports, wires, or exchanges. Just software and connectivity — which you already have.

The Cloud Makes It Even Cheaper

Our PBX systems are cloud-hosted. That means:

  • No expensive hardware to maintain onsite
  • No installation or call-out charges
  • No IT department needed to run it

You get full PBX functionality — routing, voicemail, ring groups, call forwarding — without buying a single server or rack.

Simple, Predictable Costs

With VoIP Shop, you pay for:

  • Your extensions (monthly fee)
  • Your usage (actual calls made)
  • Your hardware (once-off, or BYO)

That’s it. No mysterious line items. No legacy fees. No surprises.

Say Goodbye to the Landline Trap

Still paying R200+ a month for a line you barely use? That’s money wasted. Switching to VoIP could cut your monthly telecom bill in half — or more — while improving quality and giving your team greater flexibility.
